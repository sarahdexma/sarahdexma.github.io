馬媽媽的元寶 ✦ Mama Ma's Dumplings

To celebrate the Lunar New Year, let's make my mother's homemade dumplings! Although dumplings are normally called shuǐ jiǎo [水餃] in Chinese, during the Chinese New Year, they are called yuán bǎo [元寶], the currency of Ancient China, to represent wealth and luck. Other interpretations include: the dumpling's resemblance to the bright moon and the prosperous year or how it brings people together! Follow along below!

What you'll need!

    • 2 cups ⊹ flour
    • 1 cup ⊹ cold water
    • 2 lbs ⊹ ground pork
    • 1 ⊹ cabbage
    • 2 tbsp ⊹ soy sauce
    • 1 tbsp ⊹ salt
    • 1 tbsp ⊹ sugar
    • 1 tbsp ⊹ ginger

Now, let's make it!

  1. In a large bowl, add the flour and water to begin making the skin.
  2. Knead until everything is mixed completely & leave to rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Onto the filling! Cut the cabbage into small pieces & place it to the side.
  4. In another bowl, mix ground pork, soy sauce, salt, sugar, & ginger together.
  5. Mix well until the texture becomes stringy & sticky.
  6. Add the chopped cabbage into the bowl & continue mixing.
  7. Sprinkle some flower onto a clean, flat surface, like your counter.
  8. Roll the dough on your surface into a 1 inch thick log.
  9. Cut one inch chunks from the log & roll them into balls.
  10. Use a rolling pin to roll out the balls into flat circles, 1/8 of an inch thick.
  11. Prepare a small bowl of water to act as glue for the skin.
  12. Next, while holding one flat skin in your hand, scoop 2 tablespoons of filling.
  13. Place it in the center of the skin.
  14. Dip one finger in the bowl of water and paint it on the edge of the skin.
  15. Fold one side of the skin over the meat, touching the other side of the skin.
  16. Pinch the two sides together to seal the meat & pleat the edges.
  17. In a pot, boil water & then throw in all your dumplings.
  18. Once the dumplings float to the top, pour in one cup of water.
  19. When it begins to boil again, pour in another cup of water. Repeat once.
  20. When it comes to its last boil, they are ready!
  21. Take them out of water and serve!
  22. Optional simple dipping sauce: 2 tbsp soy sauce with 2 tbsp Chinese vinegar.
